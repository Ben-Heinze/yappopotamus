#+TITLE: ILP-Based Vertex Cover Approximation
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil

For general (non-bipartite) graphs, the LP relaxation of the Vertex
Cover ILP may return a fractional solution. Rounding that fractional
solution gives a $2$-approximation.

* The ILP and its LP relaxation

Recall the [[../integer-linear-programming/vertex-cover-ilp][Vertex Cover ILP]]:

- $x_i \in \{0, 1\}$ — indicates whether vertex $i$ is selected.
- *Objective:* $\min \sum_i x_i$.
- *Subject to:* $x_i + x_j \geq 1$ for each edge $(i, j)$.

Relaxing gives an LP with $0 \leq x_i \leq 1$ and the same edge
constraints.

* Rounding

Let $x^{LP}$ be an optimal LP solution. Round:

- If $x_i^{LP} \geq 1/2$, include vertex $i$ in the cover.
- Otherwise, exclude it.

*Validity.* For every edge $(i, j)$, the LP constraint says
$x_i^{LP} + x_j^{LP} \geq 1$, so at least one of them is $\geq 1/2$
and gets included. So every edge is covered.

* Approximation ratio

We want $ALG \leq 2 \cdot OPT$.

*Lower bound on $OPT$.* Since the LP has more feasible options than
the ILP,
\[
\sum_i x_i^{LP} \leq OPT_{ILP} = OPT.
\]

*Upper bound on $ALG$.* The algorithm's cover $S_{ALG}$ consists of
exactly the vertices with $x_i^{LP} \geq 1/2$. So

\[
ALG = |S_{ALG}| = \sum_{i \in S_{ALG}} 1
      \leq \sum_{i \in S_{ALG}} 2 x_i^{LP}
      \leq 2 \sum_i x_i^{LP}.
\]

Combining,
\[
ALG \leq 2 \sum_i x_i^{LP} \leq 2 \cdot OPT.
\]

* Why can't we do better than $1/2$?

The $1/2$ threshold is what makes rounding valid: the constraint
$x_i + x_j \geq 1$ guarantees that at least one endpoint is $\geq 1/2$.
Raising the threshold would let both endpoints slip below and leave
an edge uncovered.
