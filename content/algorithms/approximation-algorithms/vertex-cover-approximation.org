#+TITLE: Vertex Cover — 2-Approximation
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil

*Vertex Cover.* Given a graph, find the smallest subset of vertices
such that every edge in the graph has at least one endpoint in the
subset.

* Algorithm

#+BEGIN_SRC text
While an uncovered edge exists:
  select both of its endpoints
#+END_SRC

* Why $ALG \leq 2 \cdot OPT$

Let $E'$ be a subset of edges that pairwise share no vertex — a
/matching/. Then $|E'| \leq OPT$: covering each edge of $E'$ requires
at least one distinct vertex, so any vertex cover has size $\geq |E'|$.

Does the algorithm's output relate to any such matching? Yes — the
edges it processes form one. Every time the algorithm picks an
uncovered edge and adds both endpoints, subsequent iterations skip
any edge sharing either endpoint. So the picked edges form a matching
$E'$, and $ALG = 2 |E'|$.

Combining:

\[
ALG = 2 |E'| \leq 2 \cdot OPT.
\]

* Room for improvement

We cannot find optimal vertex covers in polynomial time unless
$P = NP$, but this algorithm is at worst twice optimal. Two ways to
tighten the bound:

- Design a new algorithm.
- Sharpen the analysis of this one.
