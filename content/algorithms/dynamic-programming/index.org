* Optimal substructure

/Optimal substructure/: if removing a part of an optimal solution
yields an optimal solution to a smaller sub-problem.

Central tenet of dynamic programming: /leverage optimal substructures/.

** Motivating example: trick-or-treating

Suppose the red, green, and blue houses together give you 25 pounds of
candy in the fewest possible stops, and the blue house alone gives you
5 pounds. What can we conclude?

The fastest way to collect 20 pounds is to visit the red and green
houses. If there were another color of house that gave more than 20
pounds, the original fact ("25 pounds in the fewest stops") would be
contradicted.

* The DP process

1. *Identify optimal substructure.* If we have an optimal solution of
   size $n$, what does that say about a sub-solution of size $n - i$?
2. *Recursively define the value of an optimal solution.* Express
   $A_n$ as a max or min of something involving $A_{n-i}$.
3. *Compute the value of the optimal solution.* Fill out a table.
4. *Construct the optimal solution* from the computed information —
   either by backtracking through the table, or by maintaining a
   second table alongside the values.
