#+TITLE: MST Cut Property
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil

*Lemma (Cut Property).* Suppose $S$ is a subset of the nodes of
$G = (V, E)$. Then the cheapest edge between $S$ and $V \setminus S$ is
guaranteed to be part of every MST (assuming all edge weights are unique).

- $S$: the chosen set of vertices.
- $V \setminus S$: the vertices not in $S$.

The proof is by contradiction: if a supposed MST omitted the cheapest
crossing edge, swapping it in would yield a spanning tree of strictly
smaller weight.

The cut property relies on edge weights being unique. Without unique
weights we may need a substitution argument — swap one edge from a
candidate MST with a different equally-cheap crossing edge and check
that we still get a spanning tree of the same total weight.

*Using the cut property to prove a spanning tree is minimum.* To show a
spanning tree $T$ is an MST, it suffices to show that every edge of $T$
is the cheapest edge across /some/ cut. Choosing the right $S$ for each
edge is the crux of the formal proof.
