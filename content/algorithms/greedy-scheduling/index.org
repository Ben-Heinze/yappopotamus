#+TITLE: Greedy Scheduling
#+OPTIONS: toc:2 num:nil

Three classic scheduling problems solved by greedy algorithms:

- *Interval scheduling* in a single room — pick the maximum-size subset
  of compatible courses.
- *Room minimization* — schedule every course, using as few rooms as
  possible.
- *Earliest Deadline First* — order jobs so as to minimize the maximum
  lateness across all clients.

For each we prove a greedy rule optimal by an /exchange argument/: take
any optimal schedule, and swap it towards the greedy schedule one step
at a time without increasing cost or violating validity.
