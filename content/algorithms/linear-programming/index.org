#+TITLE: Linear Programming
#+OPTIONS: toc:2 num:nil

Linear Programming is a structured way to optimize a goal by turning
knobs while following rules.

* Vocabulary

- *Objective (goal)* — what to minimize or maximize.
- *Decision variables (knobs)* — the quantities you get to choose.
- *Constraints (rules)* — restrictions on what the knobs can be.

** Everyday example

- *Goal (objective):* get as rich as possible.
- *Knobs (decision variables):* job hunting, education, risk tolerance.
- *Constraints:* no robbing, get enough sleep, see the kids more than
  once a month.

** Vertex Cover as an LP shell

- *Objective:* select smallest subset of vertices.
- *Decision variables:* /select vertex 1?/, /select vertex 2?/, ...
- *Constraints:* every edge has a selected endpoint.

* How to define a linear program

1. Figure out what the knobs are.
2. Create the objective (what to minimize or maximize).
3. Add the constraints.

* Domains for the decision variables

- Real numbers: LP is solvable in polynomial time.
- Integers: not (yet?) — that's the ILP world.

* Objective

- Can be minimized or maximized.
- Must be a linear combination of the decision variables $x_i$.

* Finding optimal solutions efficiently

Two key properties of LP optima (see lecture for full reasoning):

- The optimal value occurs at a vertex of the feasible region.
- Any local optimum is a global optimum. (This is convexity.)

*Algorithm.* Test each vertex in order until no neighbor has a larger
(or smaller) objective value.

* Example: political advertising

Choose ad spend across issues to hit voter targets in urban, suburban,
and rural regions:

| Issue          | urban | suburban | rural |
|----------------+-------+----------+-------|
| infrastructure |   -2  |    +5    |   +3  |
| gun control    |   +8  |    +2    |   -5  |
| farm subsidies |    0  |     0    |  +10  |
| gas tax        |  +10  |     0    |   -2  |

*Decision variables.*
- $x_1$ = $ spent on infrastructure ads
- $x_2$ = $ spent on gun-control ads
- $x_3$ = $ spent on farm-subsidy ads
- $x_4$ = $ spent on gas-tax ads

*Constraints.*
\[
\begin{aligned}
-2 x_1 + 8 x_2 + 10 x_4 &\geq 50{,}000 \\
5 x_1 + 2 x_2 &\geq 100{,}000 \\
3 x_1 - 5 x_2 + 10 x_3 - 2 x_4 &\geq 25{,}000 \\
x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4 &\geq 0
\end{aligned}
\]

*Objective.* Minimize total spend while covering at least half the
voters:
\[
\min\; x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + x_4
\]

* Example: wood cutting

Long boards must be cut into pieces of various lengths. Demand:

| Length (ft) | # required |
|-------------+------------|
|      7      |    276     |
|      9      |    100     |
|     12      |    250     |

*Decision variables.* Each variable is a cutting pattern (how many
boards to cut with that pattern):

- $x_1$ = boards cut 7, 7 (6' waste)
- $x_2$ = boards cut 7, 9 (4' waste)
- $x_3$ = boards cut 9, 9 (2' waste)
- $x_4$ = boards cut 7, 12 (1' waste)

(A single 12' piece would never be used.)

*Objective.*
\[
\min\; 6 x_1 + 4 x_2 + 2 x_3 + x_4
\]

*Constraints.*
\[
\begin{aligned}
2 x_1 + x_2 + x_4 &\geq 276 \\
x_2 + 2 x_3 &\geq 100 \\
x_4 &\geq 250 \\
x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4 &\geq 0
\end{aligned}
\]
